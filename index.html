How does Covid-19 affect different age groups

Since the start of the Covid-19 outbreak in Malaysia, the cases that have been reported are recorded into different age groups to provide better insights.

From this record, we see that the largest proportion of Covid-19 cases are adults, with 70.8% of the total cases. The adult age group is consistently the largest portion every month since the outbreak in Malaysia.
The second biggest portion is the children age group, with 13.8% of the total cases. However, the difference between the adult age group is rather drastic.

National COVID-19 Immunisation Programme

LINDUNG DIRI, LINDUNG SEMUA

Vaccines are a collaborative effort of the world in order to curb the Covid-19 pandemic.

Malaysia started its vaccination programme, National COVID-19 Immunisation Programme on the 24th of February. Since then, we've already achieved a partially vaccinated percentage and a fully vaccinated percentage of 75.1% and 65.6% respectively.

With great efforts from the government body of our country, our vaccination progress has been rapid.
We were able to achieve a peak of 1-2% of population vaccinated daily.


Malaysia has achieved a fully vaccinated rate of 67.8% and a partially vaccinated rate of 75.7%, leaving only 24.3% of the population not vaccinated.

With the majority of the Covid-19 cases being in major cities, the Klang Valley (consisting of Kuala Lumpur and Selangor) are being prioritised to achieve a high level of vaccination rate.

Only 8.8% of the Klang Valley population is not vaccinated, with 83.5% being fully vaccinated and 91.2% who received at least 1 dose.
